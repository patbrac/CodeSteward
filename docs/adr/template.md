## Context

Describe the problem, constraints, affected users, and why a durable decision is needed. Distinguish observed evidence from assumptions and identify the product-contract clauses involved.

## Decision

State the decision precisely, including scope, defaults, invariants, and what remains undecided. A reader should be able to tell whether an implementation conforms.

## Alternatives considered

- **Alternative:** benefits, costs, and reason not selected.
- Include “do nothing” when it is a real option.

## Security impact

Describe changed trust boundaries, untrusted inputs, execution/network capabilities, secrets, supply-chain effects, abuse cases, mitigations, and residual risk. Write `No material impact` only with a rationale.

## Privacy and data impact

Describe source/data collection, transmission, persistence, retention, deletion, logs, identifiers, and user control. State whether defaults change.

## Compatibility and cross-platform impact

Cover CLI/config/schema/API compatibility, migrations, deterministic output, native Windows/macOS/Linux behavior, filesystem/terminal/process differences, and plugin/integration impact.

## Open-core and licensing impact

State whether the decision changes the public scanner, commercial boundary, dependency licenses, contributor rights, or trademark use. A contract change requires a public RFC.

## Consequences

List benefits, costs, operational burden, failure modes, and follow-up work.

## Evidence and verification

Define falsifiable acceptance checks, fixtures, platform matrix, measurements, and evidence location. Do not mark unrun checks as passed.

## Rollout and rollback

Describe sequencing, feature/config gates, migrations, observability, failure containment, rollback conditions, and recovery. Include release-note and support implications.

## Supersession

Explain what would trigger reconsideration and the process required to replace this decision. A superseding ADR must link back here; contract changes also require an RFC.
