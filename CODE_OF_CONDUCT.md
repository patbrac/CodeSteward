# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the Code Steward community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people;
- respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences;
- giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback;
- accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience;
- focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind;
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks;
- public or private harassment;
- publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without explicit permission;
- other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces and when an individual officially represents the community in public spaces. Representation includes using an official project account, posting through an official communication channel, or acting as an appointed representative at an event.

## Reporting

For public, non-sensitive moderation requests, flag the content through GitHub and notify a maintainer without repeating harmful material. For sensitive conduct involving content on GitHub, use GitHub's private [content-reporting and abuse process](https://support.github.com/contact/report-abuse). Do not put personal or sensitive information in a public project issue or in the vulnerability-reporting form.

Project maintainers review reports they receive promptly and fairly, respect reporter privacy and safety, and share information only with people needed to respond. A maintainer named in a report must recuse from handling it. During the single-maintainer bootstrap period, the project does not claim to operate an independent confidential conduct inbox; GitHub's reporting process is the independent private route for GitHub-hosted content. The project will publish and test a separate route before promising private handling of off-platform conduct.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers follow these community-impact guidelines when determining consequences:

1. **Correction** — a private written warning for inappropriate language or other unprofessional behavior, with clarity about why it was inappropriate.
2. **Warning** — a warning with consequences for continued behavior; no interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited contact, for a specified period.
3. **Temporary ban** — a temporary ban from interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period.
4. **Permanent ban** — permanent removal for a pattern of violations, harassment, aggression, or serious harm.

Actions will be proportionate to impact, context, prior behavior, and community safety. Retaliation against a reporter or participant in an investigation is itself a violation.
